We define coordinates. Let \(y(t)\) be the vertical position, and \(v(t)\) be the downward velocity.

Two forces act on the raindrop.

Gravity: $$F_g = mg$$

acting downward.

Air Drag (For objects moving through air at moderate speeds, drag is approximately quadratic): $$F_d = \frac{1}{2}\rho C_d A v^2$$

where

\(\rho\) = air density

\(C_d\) = drag coefficient

\(A\) = cross-sectional area

Drag acts upward, opposite the motion.
