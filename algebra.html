Using Newton’s second law $$ F = ma $$ the equation of motion becomes $$ m\frac{dv}{dt} = mg - \frac{1}{2}\rho C_d A v^2 $$ This differential equation describes the velocity evolution of the raindrop. Terminal velocity occurs when acceleration becomes zero: $$ \frac{dv}{dt} = 0 $$ So the forces balance: $$ mg = \frac{1}{2}\rho C_d A v_t^2 $$

Solving for terminal velocity gives $$ v_t = \sqrt{\frac{2mg}{\rho C_d A}} $$
